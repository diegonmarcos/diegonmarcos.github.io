# Jerusalem Bagels

> Soft, sesame-covered rings of joy straight from the streets of Jerusalem. Not your NYC bagel - these are pillowy, oblong, and absolutely addictive. Best enjoyed warm with tahini.

**Yield:** 10 large bagels

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 500g flour (mix different flours - at least half white wheat, rest can be spelt 80%, rye, whole wheat)
- 1 tablespoon dry yeast (11g)
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1 flat spoon salt
- 130g yogurt
- 200ml lukewarm water + 3 tbsp

### Coating
- Sesame seeds
- Ketzach (nigella seeds)
- Flax seeds
- Sunflower/pumpkin seeds
- Olive oil
- Salt

*Vegan option: Replace yogurt with 50ml water + 4 tbsp olive oil*

## Instructions

1. Mix all dry ingredients in a large bowl
2. In another bowl, mix yeast with sugar and water until small bubbles appear
3. Pour water and yogurt mixture gently into dry ingredients
4. Mix and knead until a flexible and pleasant dough is obtained
5. Let rise for 1 to 1.5 hours
6. Preheat oven to 220Â°C (start heating 30 minutes before baking)
7. Flour a surface, roll dough and divide into 10 equal parts
8. Roll each piece into a snake shape
9. Dip quickly in water (1-2 seconds), then roll in seed mixture
10. Place on baking tray with parchment paper
11. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle salt
12. Bake for about 15 minutes (check your oven)
13. Brush with olive oil when they come out of the oven
14. Serve with tahini and pepper spread!
