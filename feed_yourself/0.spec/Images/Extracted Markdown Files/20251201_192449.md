# Noa's Cheesecake

> A proper Aussie-style cheesecake with biscuit base and berry coulis on top. The secret is letting it cool in the oven with the door ajar - it creates the perfect dip for berries. Refrigerate overnight if you can resist.

## Ingredients

### Base
- 160g Arnott's Milk Arrowroot biscuits (or similar cookies)
- 60g+ butter, melted

### Filling
- 350g cream cheese (Philadelphia), softened for 30 mins
- 2/3 cup sugar
- 2 eggs
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- A fat dash of vanilla essence
- 1 tbsp plain or tapioca flour

### Berry Coulis
- 500g frozen mixed berries
- 1 tbsp sugar
- Juice of 1/4 lemon
- 1 tsp cornstarch (add more if needed to thicken)

## Instructions

### Make the Base
1. Lightly grease and line an 18cm springform pan with baking paper
2. Crush cookies (blender, rolling pin in a ziplock bag, or food processor)
3. Mix crushed cookies with melted butter
4. Press firmly into the pan

### Make the Filling
1. Beat cream cheese and sugar with electric beater until sugar dissolves
2. Beat in eggs, one at a time, until smooth
3. Beat in sour cream, vanilla, and flour
4. Pour into prepared pan
5. Bake at 150Â°C fan forced for 1 hour to 1 hour 10 minutes until golden
6. Turn off oven, leave cheesecake inside for 15 more minutes with door ajar (this creates the perfect dip in the middle!)
7. Let cool completely
8. Refrigerate at least 2 hours (overnight is 100x better!)

### Make the Berry Coulis
1. Put frozen berries in a pot
2. Add sugar and squeeze in lemon juice
3. Heat until berries break down
4. Mix cornstarch with a little water, add to berries
5. Stir until thickened
6. Pour over the cooled cheesecake dip
