# Fatair (Middle Eastern Spinach Pies)

> Crispy pockets of herby, lemony, sumac-kissed goodness. These little triangles of joy are perfect for sharing (or not). Vegan-friendly without the feta!

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 2.5 cups (350g) flour (mix white with at least 1/3 wholemeal - spelt, whole wheat)
- 1 flat teaspoon (2g) dry yeast
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 2/3 cup (180ml) water
- 1/3 cup (80ml) olive oil + 1 tablespoon as needed
- 1 teaspoon salt

### Filling
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 2 bunches chopped winter greens (Turkish spinach, chard, wild beets)
- 1/2 cup fresh thyme leaves
- Salt and freshly ground pepper
- 2 tablespoons sumac
- 1/4 cup pine nuts
- 1/4 cup feta (optional - skip for vegan)
- Olive oil for frying

## Instructions

### Make the Dough
1. Mix flour, yeast and sugar in a wide bowl
2. Add water, olive oil and salt
3. Knead for a few minutes until smooth and flexible
4. If too sticky, add more flour (but try kneading more first, or add 1 tbsp olive oil)
5. Cover with plastic wrap or damp towel
6. Leave at room temperature for 45 minutes

### Make the Filling
1. Heat olive oil in a wide, slightly deep pan
2. Add onion and fry until golden
3. Add thyme leaves and winter greens, season with salt and pepper
4. Cook until leaves wilt and lose volume
5. Turn off heat
6. Add sumac, pine nuts, and feta if using - mix well

2. Divide dough into portions, roll out thin
