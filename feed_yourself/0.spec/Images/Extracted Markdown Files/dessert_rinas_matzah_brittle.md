# Rina's Matzah Brittle

> The Passover treat that transcends the holiday. Caramel, chocolate, and crunchy matzah in addictive harmony. You'll make it for Pesach and then mysteriously find reasons to make it year-round.

## Ingredients

- 4-5 sheets of matzah (adjust for your tray)
- 2 sticks butter
- 1 cup brown sugar
- 1 bag chocolate chips (12 ounces)
- Toppings: almonds, walnuts, salt, cranberries, etc.

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 180Â°C
2. Line a baking tray with parchment paper
3. Arrange matzah in a single layer
4. **Make the caramel:** Add butter and sugar to a pot on the stove
5. Cook until it boils, then let boil for 3 minutes (keep stirring continuously!)
6. Pour caramel over the matzah and spread with a spatula
7. Bake for 12 minutes
8. Let sit for 1 minute
9. Pour chocolate chips over the hot matzah
10. Put back in oven for 1 minute to soften the chocolate
11. Take out and spread chocolate with spatula
12. Add your toppings while chocolate is still melty
13. Leave out for 30 minutes to start setting
14. Transfer to fridge or freezer
15. Freeze for at least 1 hour
16. Break into chunks and try to share (or don't)
